Also Medical and Dental expenses over a certain % of your income maybe deductible on your tax return.<>There are several pre-tax programs similar to a Flexible Spending Account (FSA) that you may be able to take advantage of, depending on your employer and your personal circumstances. Some options include:Health Savings Account (HSA): This account is available to those with a high-deductible health plan (HDHP). Contributions to an HSA are tax-free, and the money can be used to pay for qualifying medical expenses.Dependent Care Assistance Plan (DCAP): This plan allows you to set aside pre-tax dollars to pay for child care or elderly care expenses while you are working.Transit and Parking Benefits: If your employer offers a transit and parking benefits program, you may be able to set aside pre-tax dollars to pay for public transportation or parking expenses related to your commute to work.Health Reimbursement Arrangement (HRA): An HRA is an employer-funded account that can be used to pay for out-of-pocket medical expenses, such as copays and deductibles.Commuter Benefits: Some employers offer a commuter benefits program, which allows you to set aside pre-tax dollars to pay for qualified transportation expenses, such as public transportation or vanpooling.It's important to note that these programs may have different eligibility requirements and restrictions, so it's a good idea to talk to your employer or a financial professional to determine which options may be available to you and how they can best benefit you.<>finance
