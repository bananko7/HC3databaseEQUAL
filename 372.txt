A stock lockup period is a contractual restriction that prevents insiders, such as company employees, directors, and early investors, from selling their shares in the company for a certain period of time after the company goes public. This is typically done to prevent a flood of insider selling from occurring immediately after the IPO, which could potentially cause the price of the stock to decline.During the lockup period, insiders are typically restricted from selling any of their shares in the company, with some exceptions. For example, insiders may be allowed to sell a certain number of shares after the lockup period has ended, or they may be able to sell their shares under certain circumstances, such as in the event of an emergency or to meet tax obligations.It is important to note that the terms of a stock lockup period will vary depending on the company and the specific circumstances of the IPO.
