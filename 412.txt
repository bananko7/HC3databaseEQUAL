(Note, the link is to the \"cheat sheet\" in the back of the book. The full book is worth reading.)<>Investing money is a way to potentially earn a return on your money over the long term. Here are some basic concepts to consider when it comes to investing:Diversification: It is important to diversify your investments, rather than putting all of your money into a single investment. This can help to spread risk and potentially increase your chances of earning a positive return.Risk and return: Investing always carries some level of risk, and the potential return on an investment is often proportional to the level of risk involved. Higher risk investments, such as stocks, have the potential to provide higher returns over the long term, but also come with a higher level of volatility.Time horizon: Your time horizon is the length of time you expect to hold an investment. Longer time horizons generally allow for a greater potential return, as there is more time for the investment to grow.Investment goals: It is important to have clear investment goals in mind, as this will help guide your investment decisions. Consider factors such as your risk tolerance, time horizon, and financial goals when deciding where to allocate your money.Professional advice: Seeking the advice of a financial professional can be helpful in understanding your options and making informed investment decisions.There are many different types of investments to consider, such as stocks, bonds, mutual funds, exchange-traded funds (ETFs), and real estate, among others. It is important to do your own research and understand the risks and potential rewards of any investment you are considering.<>finance
