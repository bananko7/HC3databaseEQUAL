Does a stock's price represent current liquidation of all shares?<>Is the stock's price at any given moment the price at which all shares could be sold to new investors? No.  For the simple fact that the current bid\/offer always have sizes associated. What you should be looking at is the consolidated price to buy\/sell X shares (10bn doesn't really work as not everyone is willing to sell\/buy). If you look at the spread of the consolidated price at your quantity level, you'd notice it would be in stark contrast to the spread of the best bid\/offer but (by definition) that would be the price to buy or sell X shares to new investors. Edit Calculation of the consolidated price of X shares: You go through the order book and calculate the size-weighted average price until you covered X.
