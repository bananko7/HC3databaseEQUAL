Other information about the fees charged by NYSE and NASDAQ may be found in the Investopedia article The NYSE And Nasdaq: How They Work.<>NASDAQ, or the NASDAQ Stock Market, is a stock exchange that is owned and operated by the NASDAQ OMX Group, a financial company that is listed on the NASDAQ exchange itself. NASDAQ OMX Group is a publicly traded company, so it is owned by a diverse group of shareholders who own its stock.NASDAQ operates as an electronic marketplace, where buyers and sellers can trade stocks and other securities. As an exchange, NASDAQ collects fees from the companies that list their stocks on the exchange and from the firms that facilitate trades on the exchange. These fees are used to cover the costs of operating the exchange and to generate revenue for the company.<>finance
